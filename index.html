My Favourite Recipe

A classic Victoria Sponge Cake

Ingredients

For the sponge

  • 200g caster sugar
  • 200g butter (softened)
  • 200g self-raising flour
  • 4 eggs (beaten)
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons of milk

For the filling

  • 100g butter (softened)
  • 140g icing sugar (sifted)
  • drop vanilla extract (optional)
  • half a 340g jar good-quality strawberry jam
  • Icing sugar, to decorate

Method

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 190C (of 170C for fan ovens or gas mark 5). Grease two 8-inch / 20cm sandwich tins. Line the tins with grease-proof paper.

  2. In a small bowl, beat the 4 eggs together. Add the beaten eggs to a large bowl and beat together with the caster sugar, softened butter, self-raising flour, baking powder and milk together until you have a smooth, soft batter.

  3. Divide the mixture between the sandwich tins and gently smooth the batter to give an even surface.

  4. Bake for about 20 to 25 mins until golden. Do not open the door to the oven to check before 20 minutes

  5. Turn the sponges onto a cooling rack and allow to cool completely.

  6. To make the buttercream filling, beat the remaining softened butter until smooth and creamy, then gradually beat in the sifted icing sugar and, if you like, a drop of vanilla extract.

  7. Spread the buttercream over the bottom of one of the sponges and top it with strawberry jam. Carefully place the second sponge on top.

  8. Before serving, dust with a little icing sugar to decorate.
